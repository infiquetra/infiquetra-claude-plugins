# Marketplace Pricing Framework

4-tier pricing methodology, research query templates, and Indianapolis market context.

## 4-Tier Pricing Model

Every listing gets four prices. This gives the seller flexibility based on how quickly they want to sell.

| Tier | Target vs Fair Market | Expected Sale Time | Use When |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Quick Sale** | 20-30% below | 24-48 hours | Moving soon, clearing space fast |
| **Fair Market** | Based on comps | 3-7 days | Default recommended starting price |
| **Above Market** | 10-15% above | 1-2 weeks | Patient seller, great condition |
| **Maximum Realistic** | Highest defensible | 2-4 weeks | Mint condition, rare item, high demand |

**Starting recommendation:** Always suggest Fair Market as the listing price. If no bites in 3-4 days, drop to Quick Sale. Never start at Maximum — you lose the first-48-hours traffic surge.

## WebSearch Research Queries

Use these exact queries for consistent results:

### eBay Sold Listings (best data source)
```
"[item name]" sold site:ebay.com
"[brand] [model]" completed listings ebay
```

### Local Facebook Marketplace
```
"[item name]" Facebook Marketplace Indianapolis
"[brand] [model]" "Indianapolis" for sale
```

### New Price Anchor
```
"[item name]" buy new price
"[brand] [model]" retail price
```

### Condition-Adjusted Comps
```
"[item name]" "good condition" used price
"[item name]" "like new" price sold
```

## Pricing Calculation Method

1. **Find eBay sold range** — this is the floor/ceiling anchor
2. **Check local FB comps** — Indianapolis typically runs 10-15% below eBay sold
3. **Set Fair Market** — midpoint of local FB comps (not eBay, not new)
4. **Derive other tiers:**
   - Quick Sale = Fair Market × 0.75
   - Above Market = Fair Market × 1.12
   - Maximum = Fair Market × 1.25 (or new price × 0.6, whichever is lower)

**Example:**
- eBay sold: $45-65 for Good condition
- Local FB comps: $35-50
- Fair Market: $42
- Quick Sale: $32
- Above Market: $47
- Maximum: $52

## Indianapolis Market Context

**Strong markets (price at or above comps):**
- Power tools (DeWalt, Milwaukee, Makita sell fast)
- Outdoor equipment (mowers, blowers, tillers)
- Furniture (good furniture scarce, prices firm)
- Baby/kids gear (high turnover, strong demand)
- Electronics (iPhones, gaming, audio)

**Competitive markets (price at or slightly below comps):**
- Common household items
- Books, DVDs, media
- Basic clothing
- Generic kitchen items

**Seasonal considerations:**
| Season | Hot categories |
|--------|---------------|
| Spring | Outdoor furniture, lawn equipment, bikes |
| Summer | Camping gear, pools, fans, AC units |
| Fall | Heaters, snowblowers (list early), winter gear |
| Winter | Electronics (post-holiday), exercise equipment |

**Avoid:** Don't list snowblowers in July or patio furniture in December at full price. Wait for peak season or drop 30-40% for off-season buyers.

## Item-Specific Pricing Notes

### Electronics
- Phones: 40-60% of new unlocked price (Good condition)
- Laptops: 30-50% of new (market is saturated)
- Gaming consoles: Near-MSRP when hard to find in stores; 40-60% otherwise
- CPUs/GPUs: Track current new prices carefully — used GPU market volatile
- Headphones: 40-60% of retail for well-known brands

### Tools
- Power tools: 40-60% of new; premium for complete kit with all batteries
- Hand tools: 30-50% of new; set premium if complete set
- Condition matters enormously — worn tools sell slowly at any price

### Furniture
- IKEA: 20-40% of new (buyers know the price)
- Solid wood / vintage: Can price near retail or above if genuinely unique
- Sectionals/sofas: Condition is everything; stained = near-zero value
- Bed frames: Mattresses rarely resell; frames do better without mattress listed

### Sporting Goods
- Bikes: 40-70% of retail depending on brand and condition
- Fitness equipment: Treadmills/ellipticals flood the market; price low or bundle
- Camping gear: Good margins if well-known brand (REI, Patagonia, etc.)

## Transparency Language

Always include in the listing summary when presenting pricing to the user:

> "Prices are estimates based on [eBay sold comps / local FB comps / general market knowledge]. Actual sale price will depend on condition, buyer timing, and local demand."

This sets honest expectations and protects against the user feeling misled if something sells differently.

## Negotiation Strategy

- **List 10-15% above your actual minimum** to leave room for offers
- **Add "Firm" to title** if you don't want to negotiate — reduces lowballs ~40%
- **"OBO"** invites offers but attracts lowballers
- **First 48 hours** are highest traffic — hold price firm initially
- **After 1 week** with no bites — drop to Quick Sale price and refresh the listing
- **Counter-offer strategy:** Never accept first lowball; counter at Fair Market
