# Shipping Guide for Casual Sellers

A practical guide to shipping sold items profitably when you're selling 5–15 items per month.

## Starter Kit (~$35–50 total)

Get these before shipping anything:

| Item | Cost | Where |
|------|------|-------|
| Kitchen/postal scale | $10–15 | Amazon |
| Packing tape + gun | $14–22 | Amazon or hardware store |
| Kraft paper roll (60 lb, 12" wide) | $8–12 | Amazon |
| Thermal label printer (optional) | $55–70 | Rollo or MUNBYN — worth it after 2–3 months |

**Free supplies:**
- USPS Flat Rate boxes — order free at usps.com (ships to your door)
- Regular boxes — liquor stores (strongest cardboard), Walmart after 10 PM, grocery produce departments
- Bubble wrap — save from your own deliveries; Nextdoor neighbors often give it away

## The Most Important Tool: Pirate Ship

**Pirate Ship** (pirateship.com) is free with no minimums. It gives you:
- Up to 87% off USPS retail rates
- Up to 85% off UPS daily rates
- All in one dashboard with address validation

This is non-negotiable — shipping through the post office at retail rates costs 2–3x more.

## Carrier Selection by Weight

| Item Weight | Best Option | Approx. Cost |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| Under 1 lb | USPS Ground Advantage via Pirate Ship | $3.50–5.50 |
| 1–3 lbs | USPS Ground Advantage or UPS Ground Saver | $5.50–9 |
| 3–10 lbs | Rate-shop USPS vs UPS in Pirate Ship | $9–18 |
| 10–20 lbs | UPS Ground via Pirate Ship | $15–30 |
| 20–70 lbs that fit | USPS Large Flat Rate Box ($28.70 commercial) | $28.70 flat |
| 30+ lbs that don't fit | UPS Ground; consider local-only | $30+ |

**USPS Flat Rate boxes:** If it fits, it ships for a fixed price regardless of weight (up to 70 lbs). Large Flat Rate = $28.70 at commercial rates. Unbeatable for dense items like tools or electronics.

## Dimensional Weight (DIM) Warning

Carriers charge whichever is higher: actual weight or dimensional weight.

```
DIM weight (lbs) = Length × Width × Height (inches) ÷ 139
```

Example: A 16"×12"×8" box = 1,536 ÷ 139 = **11 lbs DIM weight** — even if the item only weighs 3 lbs, you pay for 11 lbs.

**Rule:** Always use the smallest box that safely fits the item. Oversized boxes are one of the biggest cost mistakes.

## Packaging Tips

### General
1. Choose box with 2–3" clearance on all sides for padding
2. Fill all voids — item should not shift when shaken
3. Kraft paper is the cheapest void fill; crumple it well
4. Tape all seams including box edges (cross-tape heavy items)
5. Print label, attach to top, cover with clear tape to protect from moisture

### Electronics
1. **Anti-static bags** for circuit boards ($8 for 50-pack on Amazon) — essential
2. **Double-box method** for anything > $100 or fragile:
   - Inner box: item with 2" padding all sides
   - Outer box: inner box with 2" padding all sides
   - Costly but prevents damage claims
3. **Remove batteries** before shipping (some carriers require it)
4. Include all cables and accessories shown in listing photos

### Fragile Items
- Always use "Fragile" sticker (limited protection but good practice)
- Bubble wrap individual fragile components
- Rigid foam corners for screens/panels
- Mark the correct "This Side Up" orientation

## Shipping Cost Estimation for Pricing

When pricing an item you'll ship, estimate shipping cost first:

| Weight Estimate | Shipping Est. (Pirate Ship) | Add to Item Price |
|----------------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| Under 1 lb | $4–6 | Usually buyer pays |
| 1–3 lbs | $6–9 | Usually buyer pays |
| 3–8 lbs | $9–15 | Buyer pays or add to price |
| 8–20 lbs | $15–28 | Add to price or list local |

For Mercari: buyer pays shipping separately via the platform's prepaid label system. You don't bake it into the price.

For FB Marketplace with shipping: either add shipping to the price and write "ships for free," or state "buyer pays actual shipping."

## Common Mistakes

1. **Using retail USPS rates** — Always use Pirate Ship; this alone can save $5–15 per shipment
2. **Oversized box** — DIM weight penalty can triple the shipping cost
3. **Underestimating weight** — Weigh on a scale; guessing leads to postage due
4. **Not tracking** — Always ship with tracking; it protects you from "never received" claims
5. **No insurance on high-value items** — Add insurance for anything > $100 (Pirate Ship makes this easy)
6. **Shipping batteries without checking rules** — Lithium batteries have special rules; check before shipping

## Quick Decision: To Ship or Not?

Ask these questions:

1. Does it weigh under 10 lbs? → Shipping is viable
2. Is the sale price > $25? → Shipping economics work
3. Can it be safely packaged? → Proceed

If all three: **ship**. Otherwise: **local pickup only**.

At $25 sale price with $7 shipping, you net $18 before fees. That's still fine for something that might not sell locally. At $15 sale price with $7 shipping, you net $8 — probably not worth it.
